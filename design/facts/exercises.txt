# Muscle group 

A muscle group has a priority.

Chest has priority high.
Back has priority high.
Shoulder has priority high.
Quads has priority high.
Hamstring has priority high.
Glutes has priority high.
Biceps has priority low.
Triceps has priority low.
Calves has priority low.
Abs has priority low.

# Sub-muscle group (A muscle group can be divided into smaller groups)

A sub-muscle group has a priority within the main muscle group.

Middle chest has high priority.
Upper chest has medium priority.
Lower chest has low priority.

Frontal-plane lats has high priority.
Upper back has high priority.
Sagittal-plane lats has low priority.

Front delt has high priority.
Side delt has medium priority.
Rear delt has low priority.

Biceps long and short head has high priority.
Biceps branchialis has low priority.

Triceps lateral and medial head has high priority.
Triceps long head has low priority.

Quadriceps exercise has high priority.
Rectus femoris has low priority.

Hamstring (lengthened) exercises has high priority.
Hamstring (shortened) exercises has low priority.

Gluteus maximus exercises have high priority.
Gluteus medius exercises have low priority.
Gluteus minimus exercises have low priority.

Gastrocnemius has high priority.

Rectus abdominis has high priority.

# Exercise

An exercise has a name.
An exercise has a targeted muscle group.
An exercise has a targeted sub-muscle group.
An exercise is either a compound or isolation movement.
An exercise is either a freeweight or a machine exercise.
